🧠 We Know What That Pile Feels Like

You're not imagining it. eBay listing genuinely is harder when you're ADHD or autistic. Not because you're less capable — because the task is badly designed for how your brain works.

  • 😶 Too many micro-decisions at once. Category. Condition. Postage type. Duration. Collection only or not? Each one is a small context switch that drains executive function you don't have spare.
  • 🔁 The task has no clear start. ADHD brains need a clear first step. "List this thing" isn't a step. It's a project. So you open the app, stare at it, and put it down.
  • 📝 Writing descriptions is agonising. The blank text box. The pressure to sound professional. The fear of missing something that will get you a bad review. Autistic perfectionism and ADHD avoidance, tag-teaming against you.
  • ⏱️ No dopamine until it's done. And the task is never done because you never start. The pile grows. The guilt grows. You feel worse about something you're perfectly capable of.
"The friction isn't you. The friction is bad design. SpicyLister removes it."
