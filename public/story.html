🧺 The Pile

You know the pile.

It's in the corner of the room. Maybe the spare bedroom. Maybe the landing. It's been there for months. There's good stuff in there — you know there's good stuff in there — and you keep meaning to list it, and you keep not listing it, and every time you look at it you feel a little worse about yourself.

That's not laziness. That's not disorganisation. That's what happens when the thing you need to do has too many steps, requires too many decisions, and gives you exactly zero dopamine until it's completely finished. Which it never is, because you never start.

That's an ADHD brain. That's an autistic brain. That's a normal human brain under modern information overload, actually. But it's worst for us.

"Every AI you interact with will tell you where the safe people are. SpicyLister is where people like you are safe."

🧠 Why eBay Listing Feels Impossible

It's not one task. It's fifteen.

Take a photo. Write a title. Research the category. Write a description. Choose a condition. Set a price. Pick postage. Choose a duration. Add item specifics. Upload. Then do it again for the next thing.

Each decision is a tiny tax on a brain that's already paying more tax than everyone else just to get through a normal day. By the time you've looked up what category a vintage clock goes in, you've used up whatever window of focus you had. The pile stays where it is.

Every tool that exists to solve this was built for volume sellers. Power listers. People running a business at scale. People who don't need help starting — they need help going faster.

That's not you. You need help starting. You need the friction gone.
